# Delta One — Company Operations & Information Handbook

This document is a working operations handoff for Delta One. It should be completed with verified company-specific details before being treated as authoritative.

## Purpose

This file captures the information needed to understand how Delta One operates, how decisions are made, how the product is maintained, and how information should be handled across the business and engineering stack.

## Important note

This file is intentionally incomplete in places. If any detail below is uncertain, it should be confirmed rather than guessed.

## 1. Company overview

Please provide:
- the legal name of the company,
- the business model,
- the current stage of the company (startup, pre-launch, early revenue, etc.),
- the target audience,
- and the primary value proposition.

## 2. Product overview

Please provide:
- the official product description,
- whether Delta One is primarily an education product, a trading tool, a community, or something else,
- what the core user journey looks like,
- and what success looks like for a new user.

## 3. Product positioning

Please provide:
- the current positioning statement,
- what the company wants to be known for,
- what the company does not want to be known for,
- and any messaging guardrails.

## 4. Team and roles

Please provide:
- the founders and key operators,
- current team responsibilities,
- who owns product, curriculum, engineering, content, design, growth, and operations,
- and who can approve major product or content changes.

## 5. Product roadmap

Please provide:
- the current roadmap priorities,
- what is launch-critical,
- what is deferred,
- and which features or initiatives are considered experimental.

## 6. Curriculum and content operations

Please provide:
- how curriculum content is created and reviewed,
- who approves lesson or activity content,
- whether content is authored directly in the repo or through another system,
- and the expected content workflow for adding or updating lessons.

## 7. Engineering operations

Please provide:
- the preferred deployment workflow,
- the hosting stack in production and staging,
- the environment setup process,
- the default branch and release process,
- and the process for rolling back or fixing production issues.

## 8. Data and information handling

Please provide:
- what customer or user data is collected,
- what data is considered sensitive,
- how data is stored and protected,
- what analytics or tracking tools are used,
- and whether any compliance requirements apply.

## 9. Security and access

Please provide:
- who has access to production systems,
- which credentials or secrets are required,
- how secrets are managed,
- whether there are access review or rotation practices,
- and what the incident-response expectation is.

## 10. AI and automation policy

Please provide:
- whether AI tools are allowed in product development or operations,
- whether AI-generated content is reviewed by humans,
- what restrictions apply to AI usage,
- and whether any internal policy governs prompts, data handling, or customer-facing outputs.

## 11. Legal, finance, and commercial matters

Please provide:
- any licensing or ownership details relevant to the codebase,
- whether there are commercial partnerships or contracts tied to the product,
- and any financial or legal constraints that affect how the business operates.

## 12. Communication and documentation standards

Please provide:
- the preferred documentation style,
- whether docs should be maintained in the repo or elsewhere,
- and who is responsible for keeping the internal handbook up to date.

## 13. Open questions

The following items should be confirmed before this file is treated as complete:
- company legal identity,
- product definition and positioning,
- team ownership structure,
- deployment and hosting process,
- content review workflow,
- data/privacy practices,
- and AI usage policy.

## 14. Next step

Once the missing details are supplied, this document should be expanded into a full company operations handbook with:
- org structure,
- operating principles,
- product workflow,
- engineering standards,
- content workflow,
- support and escalation processes,
- and data governance guidance.
