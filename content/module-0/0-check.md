# Module 0 Check

## What you should be able to do now

After completing Module 0, you should be able to:
- Explain what a stock is and what owning a share actually means
- Describe how a stock exchange works and why prices change
- Identify what bulls and bears represent, and how market sentiment shapes price
- Read a basic price chart and recognize the difference between a trend and noise
- Look at a price move on a real stock and form a hypothesis about what caused it

## Quick questions

Answer these in your head (or write them out):

1. A company has 1,000,000 shares outstanding. You own 5,000. What percentage of the company do you own?
2. What is the difference between a market order and a limit order?
3. A stock drops 8% in one day with no news. What are three possible explanations?
4. What does it mean when the VIX is high?
5. NVDA has strong earnings but the stock drops after the report. Why might that happen?

## Chart task

Pull up any major index (SPY, QQQ, or DJI) on a free charting tool like TradingView or Yahoo Finance. Look at the past 6 months.

- Identify at least one period where the market was trending up
- Identify at least one period where it was choppy or sideways
- Pick one large single-day move and try to find the news that caused it

You don’t need to log this anywhere — just practice the habit of connecting price to cause.

## Before you move on

Module 0 gave you the foundation. You now understand what the market is, what you’re actually buying when you buy a stock, and the basic forces that move prices. That’s the floor everything else gets built on.

Module 1 goes deeper: you’ll learn how to read a company’s financials, understand valuation, and start building the analytical framework that separates informed traders from noise traders.
