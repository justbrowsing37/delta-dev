# Activity 0.1A — Calculate Your Ownership Percentage

## What you're doing
You're going to calculate exactly how much of NVDA you would own with a $1,000 investment — and put a real number on what "part-owner" means.

## What you need
- Any stock screener or brokerage platform that shows NVDA's current share price and market cap (Yahoo Finance, Google Finance, or TradingView all work)

## Your task
1. Look up NVDA's current share price and total market cap
2. Calculate how many shares $1,000 buys you (it'll be a fraction — that's expected)
3. Divide your share count by the total shares outstanding to get your ownership percentage — write it out in full (e.g. 0.000003%)

## What to look for
> **Don't skip the percentage.** The point isn't that the number is small — it's that it's *real*. You would own a legally enforceable fraction of one of the most valuable companies on earth. That fraction entitles you to a proportional share of every dollar NVDA earns from now on. The number being small doesn't make it symbolic.
