# Activity 0.2A — Analyse META's Last Buyback

## What you're doing
You're going to find a real META share buyback announcement, calculate how it changed per-share ownership, and connect it to the ownership framing from Lesson 0.2.

## What you need
- META's investor relations page or a financial news search for "META share buyback" (investor.fb.com or search Google Finance / Yahoo Finance)
- A calculator

## Your task
1. Find META's most recent share buyback announcement — note the dollar amount and the approximate share price at the time
2. Estimate how many shares were repurchased (buyback amount ÷ share price)
3. Using your Activity 0.1A ownership percentage as a baseline: if META did an equivalent buyback, how would your ownership % change? Calculate the before and after.

## What to look for
> **The price jumped on the announcement, not the completion.** Buybacks take months to execute, but the stock moved the day it was announced. That's the market pricing in the future ownership concentration immediately. This is the same mechanic from Lesson 0.1 — price moves on *expectation*, not current reality.
