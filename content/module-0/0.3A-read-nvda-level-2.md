# Activity 0.3A — Read NVDA's Level 2 Order Book

## What you're doing
You're going to pull up a live Level 2 view of NVDA and identify the real-time structure of the order book — the thing that actually determines price.

## What you need
- A platform with Level 2 data: ThinkorSwim (free), Webull (free), or TradingView (paid, but free trial works)
- Do this during market hours (9:30am–4:00pm ET) for a live book — outside hours you'll see the pre/post-market book which is much thinner

## Your task
1. Open NVDA's Level 2 view — you'll see two columns: bids (buy orders) on the left, asks (sell orders) on the right
2. Identify the best bid and best ask — calculate the current spread in dollars and as a percentage of the share price
3. Look at the top 3 bid levels — note the price and the size (number of shares) at each level

## What to look for
> **Watch what happens when a large market order hits.** If you see the bid stack suddenly thin out and the price drop a few cents, that's market impact in real time — a seller ate through multiple bid levels at once. This is the mechanic from Lesson 0.3, live.
