# What Moves a Stock Price?

## The idea in one sentence
Stock prices move when the balance between buying pressure and selling pressure shifts — and that shift is driven by changing beliefs about a company's future value.

## Why this matters
If you don't understand what moves prices, you're just watching a line go up and down. Understanding the forces behind price action is what separates a trader who can reason about the market from one who just reacts to it. Everything Delta One does is built on understanding these forces at the institutional level.

## How it works
At the most mechanical level, prices move because of order flow imbalance. If more buy orders arrive than sell orders at the current price, sellers can charge more — the price rises. If more sell orders arrive, buyers can demand a discount — the price falls.

But what drives those orders? At the retail level: news, earnings, analyst upgrades, hype, fear. At the institutional level: macro positioning, sector rotation, options hedging, and systematic fund flows. The institutional layer dwarfs retail — a single large fund rebalancing its portfolio can move a mid-cap stock by several percent with no news at all.

Macro events hit prices differently. An interest rate decision from the Fed doesn't change NVDA's products or customers — but it changes the discount rate used to value future earnings, which changes what every institution is willing to pay for every stock simultaneously. That's why macro days can move markets with no company-specific news.

> **Key concept:** Most retail-visible price moves (earnings, news) are just the visible surface. The deeper driver is institutional order flow — large players repositioning their portfolios based on changing risk and return calculations.

## What this looks like in the market
In late 2022, META's stock fell from $380 to $88 — a 77% drop. The company was still profitable. The fall was driven by rising interest rates (making future earnings worth less), a slowdown in ad revenue growth, and the market repricing the risk of Zuckerberg's metaverse bet. Three institutional-level factors, no product failure.

Then in 2023, META's "year of efficiency" triggered one of the largest single-year recoveries in large-cap history. The product didn't change. The market's belief about future earnings and management discipline did.

## Common mistake
Beginners look for *a reason* for every price move. Most intraday moves don't have a news catalyst — they're the result of institutional order flow, options market maker hedging, or systematic strategy triggers. Trying to explain every candle with a headline will mislead you.
