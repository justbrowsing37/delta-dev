# Activity 0.4A — Find an Unexplained NVDA Price Move

## What you're doing
You're going to find a real NVDA price move from the last 6 months that had no obvious news catalyst, and explain what likely drove it using the order flow logic from Lessons 0.3 and 0.4.

## What you need
- NVDA's daily chart on TradingView or Yahoo Finance (set to 6-month view)
- A news search tool (Google, Yahoo Finance news tab, or Benzinga) to check whether news existed on a given date

## Your task
1. Scan NVDA's daily chart for a candle with above-average range (a notably large up or down day)
2. Search for news on that specific date — confirm there was no earnings release, no major analyst action, no macro event
3. Write 2–3 sentences explaining what likely caused the move using at least one of these concepts: order flow imbalance, institutional repositioning, options hedging, or market impact from a large order

## What to look for
> **"No news" is not the same as "no cause."** Every price move has a cause — you just can't always see it on the surface. The ability to reason about invisible institutional activity from visible price action is one of the core skills this curriculum builds toward. This activity is your first attempt at it.
