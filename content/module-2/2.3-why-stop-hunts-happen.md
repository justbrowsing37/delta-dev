# Why stop hunts happen
## The idea in one sentence
Price often moves to trigger stops because stops are easy liquidity targets.
## Why this matters
Knowing this helps you avoid falling into the trap of obvious levels.
## How it works
Stop orders cluster near obvious lows and highs, and larger players can use them to create liquidity for their own trades.
> Key concept: A stop hunt is a move aimed at the people who placed their stops in obvious spots.
## What this looks like in the market
NVDA can dip below a visible swing low, then reverse sharply as stop orders are swept.
## Common mistake
Assuming every sweep is a reversal signal instead of a possible liquidity grab.
