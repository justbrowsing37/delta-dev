# Liquidity sweeps explained
## The idea in one sentence
A liquidity sweep is a move that pushes price past a level to collect orders.
## Why this matters
Sweeps often set up the next real move by clearing weak positions.
## How it works
Price extends beyond a support or resistance level, draws in stops, then reverses or continues with the new order flow.
> Key concept: A sweep is not a trade signal on its own; it is a way the market fills orders.
## What this looks like in the market
NVDA may spike past a low and then rally, or it may continue lower after the sweep.
## Common mistake
Seeing every wick as a fakeout instead of recognizing the liquidity sweep process.
