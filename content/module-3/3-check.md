# Module 3 Check
## What you should be able to do now
- Explain why price does not move in straight lines.
- Identify obvious levels and why price returns to them.
- Recognize an engineered move and the traps it can create.
- Decide which side was likely trapped in a trade example.
## Quick questions
- What does an engineered move try to accomplish?
- Why is the obvious trade often the riskiest?
- How can a sweep collect liquidity before a move?
## Chart task
Find a real move where price looked wrong before it went right, mark the obvious levels, and explain the trapped side.
## Before you move on
If you cannot describe why a move was engineered, revisit the lessons on obvious levels and liquidity.
