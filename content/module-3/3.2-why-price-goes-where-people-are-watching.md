# Why price goes where people are watching
## The idea in one sentence
Price often moves toward the levels that attract the most attention and orders.
## Why this matters
This explains why obvious levels can still be important even when they feel easy.
## How it works
Many participants place orders around the same obvious points, which concentrates liquidity and draws price there.
> Key concept: The market is not random; it is shaped by where participants expect price to go.
## What this looks like in the market
A well-known swing high or low can act as a magnet for price revisits.
## Common mistake
Thinking obvious levels are unimportant because too many people know them.
