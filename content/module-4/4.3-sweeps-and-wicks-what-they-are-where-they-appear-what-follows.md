# Sweeps and wicks — what they are, where they appear, what follows
## The idea in one sentence
A wick often shows a liquidity sweep or a rejection of a price area.
## Why this matters
Wicks can tell you where the market tested and rejected orders.
## How it works
Price extends to a level, leaves a tail, and then moves away, revealing where supply or demand was strong.
> Key concept: Not every wick is a signal; the context determines its meaning.
## What this looks like in the market
NVDA can show a long lower wick after a test of support, then rally back up.
## Common mistake
Claiming every wick is a reversal signal rather than a test or liquidity sweep.
