# Rebalancing — when and why
## The idea in one sentence
Rebalancing adjusts your portfolio back to your intended targets.
## Why this matters
It keeps your risk levels consistent and forces discipline into the long game.
## How it works
When one holding grows faster than others, you sell some and buy the laggards to restore balance.
> Key concept: Rebalancing is a risk-management step, not a market-timing move.
## What this looks like in the market
After a strong run in one sector, you may reduce exposure and add to another area.
## Common mistake
Rebalancing too often or in response to noise instead of real allocation drift.
