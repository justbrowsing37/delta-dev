# Entry, stop, and target placement on swing timeframes
## The idea in one sentence
Good swing trades have a clear entry, stop, and target on the daily or 4H chart.
## Why this matters
It defines the risk and the potential reward before you commit.
## How it works
You use structure and pattern to place the entry, then set a stop below invalidation and a target based on the next level.
> Key concept: The risk-reward plan should fit the timeframe and the setup.
## What this looks like in the market
A long swing trade may use a breakout entry, a stop below support, and a target at the next resistance.
## Common mistake
Putting the stop too tight because the timeframe can include normal noise.
