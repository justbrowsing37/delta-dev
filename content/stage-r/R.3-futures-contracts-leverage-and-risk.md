# Futures — contracts, leverage, and risk
## The idea in one sentence
Futures are contracts to buy or sell an asset at a future date, often with leverage.
## Why this matters
Leverage can amplify returns and losses, so risk management is essential.
## How it works
You control a large amount of the underlying asset with a smaller margin deposit.
> Key concept: Futures are not the same as owning the underlying asset; they are a leveraged contract.
## What this looks like in the market
Futures markets include stock index futures, commodity futures, and interest rate futures.
## Common mistake
Ignoring the leverage and margin requirements in futures.
