8 Modules · 4 Stages · Full Foundation + Path System
Last updated: June 28, 2026

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
OVERVIEW
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Delta Core is structured in two layers:

  FOUNDATION (Modules 0–5) — Hard gated. Every module must be completed
  before the next one unlocks. The learner earns their way through.

  OPEN LAYER (Module 6 + Stages A/B/C/R) — Unlocked after completing the
  foundation. Soft gated. The learner chooses their path and moves at
  their own pace.

Everything is free at launch.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
GATING LOGIC
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Modules 0–5   HARD GATE — module check must be completed to unlock next
  Module 6      SOFT GATE — path selection required to unlock stages
  Stages A/B/C  SOFT GATE — learner progresses at own pace
  Stage R       UNLOCKED for everyone upon completing Module 6

  Each module ends with a CHECK item. The check format is:
    - What you should be able to do now (3–4 bullets)
    - Quick questions (3–5 short prompts requiring genuine thought)
    - Chart task (one real-chart application prompt)
    - Before you move on (honest self-assessment — if you can't do X, go back)

  At launch: self-reported completion. Post-launch Pro feature: AI feedback
  on check responses via Groq or equivalent.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CONTENT FORMAT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Pattern: Lesson → Activity → Lesson → Activity → ... → Check
  Each activity builds on the output of the previous activity.
  Each module check threads back to concepts from earlier modules.

  Lesson template:
    # [Title]
    ## The idea in one sentence
    ## Why this matters
    ## How it works
    > Key concept: [...]
    ## What this looks like in the market
    ## Common mistake
    ## Next

  Activity template:
    # Activity [X.XA] — [Title]
    ## What you're doing
    ## What you need
    ## Your task
    ## What to look for
    ## Connects to

  Check template:
    # Module X Check
    ## What you should be able to do now
    ## Quick questions
    ## Chart task
    ## Before you move on

  File naming:
    Lessons:    X.Y-short-title.md        e.g. 0.1-what-is-a-stock.md
    Activities: X.YA-short-title.md       e.g. 0.1A-calculate-ownership.md
    Checks:     X-check.md                e.g. 0-check.md

  Tone: plain English, second person, active voice, no fluff, no advice language.
  Examples: use NVDA or META as default tickers. No hypothetical companies.
  Target length: 400–550 words per lesson (3–4 min read).

FOUNDATION — MODULES 0–5 (HARD GATED)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MODULE 0 — What is the Market?  (~10 items)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Goal: Complete beginner understands what they are looking at.
        No assumed knowledge. No jargon without explanation.
  Unlocks: Module 1

  0.1   LESSON    What is a stock?
  0.1A  ACTIVITY  Calculate your ownership % from a $1,000 NVDA buy
  0.2   LESSON    What does it mean to own a share?
  0.2A  ACTIVITY  Analyse META's last buyback — calculate ownership shift
  0.3   LESSON    How does a stock exchange work?
  0.3A  ACTIVITY  Read NVDA's Level 2 order book — identify spread and bids
  0.4   LESSON    What moves a stock price?
  0.4A  ACTIVITY  Find an NVDA move with no news — explain via order flow
  0.5   LESSON    Bulls, bears, and market sentiment
  0.C   CHECK     Module 0 check — chart task + reflection


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MODULE 1 — Reading the Market  (~9 items)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Goal: Learner can read a price chart and understand what they are seeing.
        Introduces candlesticks, ranges, support/resistance, and breakouts.
  Threads back: Module 0 price movement concepts
  Unlocks: Module 2

  1.1   LESSON    How to read a price chart
  1.1A  ACTIVITY  Label open/close/high/low on 3 candles of NVDA daily
  1.2   LESSON    What is a trading range?
  1.2A  ACTIVITY  Draw the current trading range on your 1.1A chart
  1.3   LESSON    Support and resistance
  1.3A  ACTIVITY  Add S/R lines to your 1.2A chart — mark tested levels
  1.4   LESSON    Breakouts vs fakeouts
  1.4A  ACTIVITY  Identify one breakout and one fakeout on your annotated chart
  1.C   CHECK     Module 1 check — chart task + reflection


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MODULE 2 — How Big Money Moves  (~11 items)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Goal: Learner understands the difference between retail and institutional
        thinking. Introduces liquidity as a concept.
  Threads back: Module 1 ranges and breakouts
  Unlocks: Module 3

  2.1   LESSON    Retail traders vs institutional traders
  2.1A  ACTIVITY  Compare NVDA volume on a normal day vs earnings day
  2.2   LESSON    What is liquidity?
  2.2A  ACTIVITY  Circle 3 liquidity clusters on your Module 1 chart
  2.3   LESSON    Why stop hunts happen
  2.3A  ACTIVITY  Find a stop hunt candle — mark stops, sweep, reversal
  2.4   LESSON    Liquidity sweeps explained
  2.4A  ACTIVITY  Classify your 2.3A candle: sweep or genuine breakout?
  2.5   LESSON    Reading the 4H range
  2.5A  ACTIVITY  Switch to 4H — identify range high/low, mark position
  2.C   CHECK     Module 2 check — chart task + reflection


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MODULE 3 — How Price Really Behaves  (~9 items)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Goal: Learner builds conceptual intuition for why price behaves
        counterintuitively. Concepts introduced first — terminology follows
        from understanding, not the other way around.
  Threads back: Module 1 ranges, Module 2 institutional behavior
  Unlocks: Module 4

  NOTE FOR CONTRIBUTORS: Do not lead with vocabulary (stop hunt, reversal).
  Build the intuition first. The learner should understand the concept
  before they learn what it is called.

  3.1   LESSON    Price doesn't move in straight lines
  3.1A  ACTIVITY  Trace a real move — find where it looked wrong before it went right
  3.2   LESSON    Why price goes where people are watching
  3.2A  ACTIVITY  Mark the obvious levels on a chart — observe where price visited
  3.3   LESSON    The engineered move
  3.3A  ACTIVITY  Find a move that extended just past a key level then reversed
  3.4   LESSON    Why the obvious trade is often wrong
  3.4A  ACTIVITY  Three examples — identify which side got trapped and why
  3.C   CHECK     Module 3 check — chart task + reflection
                  (threads back to range structure from Module 1)


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MODULE 4 — Pattern Box  (~9 items)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Goal: Learner builds a visual vocabulary. Each lesson introduces one
        pattern — what it looks like, which timeframes it appears on, what
        its side effects are, and what it is commonly mistaken for.
        No execution. No entry signals. Recognition only.
  Threads back: Module 3 conceptual intuition
  Unlocks: Module 5

  NOTE FOR CONTRIBUTORS: This is a reference module. The learner is building
  a toolkit for recognition, not a trading plan. Each lesson should include
  a clear "what this is NOT" section to address the most common misread.

  4.1   LESSON    Ranges and consolidation — what they look like and why they form
  4.1A  ACTIVITY  Find and label a range on a current chart
  4.2   LESSON    Breakouts and false breakouts — visual differences and timeframe context
  4.2A  ACTIVITY  Spot the false break — find one on NVDA or META
  4.3   LESSON    Sweeps and wicks — what they are, where they appear, what follows
  4.3A  ACTIVITY  Find a wick sweep on a real chart — label the sweep and what came after
  4.4   LESSON    Trend structure — higher highs, lower lows, and structural shifts
  4.4A  ACTIVITY  Map a trend shift — find where structure changed direction
  4.C   CHECK     Module 4 check — chart task + reflection


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MODULE 5 — Reading a Setup  (~9 items)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Goal: Learner assembles everything from Modules 0–4 into a coherent read.
        This is the foundation capstone. After this module the product opens.
        The standard is understanding, not execution-readiness.
  Threads back: All previous modules — every item should reference prior concepts
  Unlocks: Module 6 (the fork) + completion badge

  NOTE FOR CONTRIBUTORS: Module 5 is the hardest to write well because it
  must genuinely integrate all prior content. Activities here should require
  the learner to explain their reasoning, not just identify a pattern.
  5.3A is the capstone activity — treat it as a mini-assessment of the
  entire foundation curriculum.

  5.1   LESSON    What makes a setup valid — confluence, not one signal
  5.1A  ACTIVITY  Build a confluence checklist using concepts from all prior modules
  5.2   LESSON    Reading context before the chart — macro backdrop, session, structure
  5.2A  ACTIVITY  Context first — given a chart, write the context read before identifying any pattern
  5.3   LESSON    Putting a read together — walk through a real example end to end
  5.3A  ACTIVITY  Write your own read — full plain-English analysis of a new chart
                  (structure, pattern, confluence, what confirms, what invalidates)
  5.4   LESSON    When to stand aside — recognizing ambiguity and doing nothing
  5.4A  ACTIVITY  The no-trade journal — find three charts where not trading was right
  5.C   CHECK     Module 5 check — chart task + reflection
                  COMPLETION MOMENT: learner has finished the foundation.
                  UX should acknowledge this explicitly before opening Module 6.

THE FORK — MODULE 6 (SOFT GATED)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MODULE 6 — Choose Your Path  (~8 items)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Goal: Learner selects the trading style that fits their goals, time
        commitment, and personality. Product responds by surfacing the
        relevant stage. All paths are presented neutrally — no path is
        better than another.
  Unlocks: Stage A, B, or C (based on selection) + Stage R (for everyone)

  NOTE FOR CONTRIBUTORS: 6.1 must be completely neutral. Do not imply that
  day trading is more sophisticated or that ETF investing is boring. Each
  path has different demands and different outcomes. Present them honestly.

  6.1   LESSON    What kind of trader do you want to be — honest breakdown of all paths
  6.2   LESSON    Path A — The long game (ETF, position trading, macro cycles)
  6.2A  ACTIVITY  Build a sample long-term allocation using index ETFs
  6.3   LESSON    Path B — The swing (daily/4H application, multi-day holds)
  6.3A  ACTIVITY  Find a swing setup using Module 5 skills
  6.4   LESSON    Path C — The active trader (intraday, session structure, execution discipline)
  6.4A  ACTIVITY  Map an intraday session on NVDA — identify session structure
  6.C   CHECK     Path selection — learner declares their chosen path
                  (not a quiz — a genuine decision prompt with written reflection)

OPEN LAYER — STAGES (SOFT GATED, UNLOCKED AFTER MODULE 6)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
STAGE A — ETF and Long-Term Investing
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  For: Learners who chose Path A in Module 6.
  Goal: Apply the foundation concepts at a higher timeframe and lower
        frequency. Understand macro cycles, index structure, sector rotation,
        rebalancing, and position trading.

  Items to be defined by contributors.
  Suggested scope:
    - What is an ETF and how does it work
    - Index investing vs stock picking
    - Macro cycles — expansion, contraction, recovery
    - Sector rotation — how money moves between sectors
    - Position trading — holding through volatility
    - Rebalancing — when and why
    - Building a simple long-term portfolio
    - Stage A check


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
STAGE B — Swing Trading
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  For: Learners who chose Path B in Module 6.
  Goal: Apply foundation concepts at the daily and 4H level. Multi-day
        to multi-week holds. Pattern recognition in context. Hold management.

  Items to be defined by contributors.
  Suggested scope:
    - Swing trading defined — holding period, time commitment, realistic expectations
    - Applying Module 4 patterns at the daily level
    - Identifying swing setups using Module 5 confluence framework
    - Entry, stop, and target placement on swing timeframes
    - Hold management — when to stay in, when to exit early
    - Managing positions around news and earnings
    - Building a simple swing trading process
    - Stage B check


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
STAGE C — Intraday and Day Trading
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  For: Learners who chose Path C in Module 6.
  Goal: Apply foundation concepts at the 1H and below. Session structure,
        execution discipline, intraday range reading, the mental demands
        of high-frequency decisions.

  Items to be defined by contributors.
  Suggested scope:
    - Day trading defined — session commitment, capital requirements, realistic expectations
    - Session structure — London open, New York open, overlap, close
    - Intraday range reading — applying Module 4 patterns at 15M and 5M
    - Execution — entries, stops, targets on short timeframes
    - The mental demands of intraday trading — patience, discipline, not overtrading
    - Common intraday mistakes and how to avoid them
    - Building a simple intraday process
    - Stage C check


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
STAGE R — Resources  (unlocked for all learners after Module 6)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  For: Everyone — regardless of path chosen.
  Goal: Give the learner an honest map of the full trading world and
        the best external resources for going deeper. Curated, not exhaustive.
        No execution teaching here — this is reference and exploration only.

  NOTE: Stage R has no activities. It is a reference layer.
        No fluff. No affiliate framing. Just honest, useful pointers.

  R.1   LESSON    The full trading landscape — holding periods, asset classes, the full map
  R.2   LESSON    Forex — what it is, how it works, who it is for
  R.3   LESSON    Futures — contracts, leverage, and risk
  R.4   LESSON    Options — rights, obligations, and why complexity matters
  R.5   LESSON    Crypto — volatility, market structure, and speculation
  R.6   REFERENCE Tools and platforms worth knowing — charting, brokers, screeners
  R.7   REFERENCE Books and resources worth reading — curated list with honest context
  R.8   REFERENCE Glossary — living document, all terms used across the curriculum
