# Product Vision

## What Delta One is

Delta One is a market education product built around structured learning.

The core user promise is not "we tell you what to trade."
The promise is: we help you understand how markets move, how price behaves, and how to think more clearly about what you are seeing.

At launch, the public-facing product is Delta Core:
- lessons,
- activities,
- progress,
- and a coherent learning path.

## What Delta One is not

Delta One is not currently being positioned as:
- a public signal platform,
- a live bot dashboard product,
- an AI-first assistant,
- or a get-rich-quick tool.

Those framings create the wrong expectations and pull the build in the wrong direction.

## Current priority order

1. Curriculum depth and quality.
2. Lesson/activity delivery system.
3. Contributor onboarding and documentation.
4. Stable app structure and hosting.
5. Bot maintenance in the background.
6. AI only if it becomes helpful later.

## Role of the bot

The bot still matters, but not as the center of the user-facing product.

For now, the bot is:
- private infrastructure,
- a research tool,
- and a potential future source of examples or case studies.

It should not dominate the roadmap, product copy, or contributor workflow.

## Role of AI

AI is explicitly deprioritized.

It may later help with:
- explanation layers,
- review support,
- lesson assistance,
- or guided exploration.

But none of that is required for launch.
If AI adds complexity before the curriculum is strong, it is a distraction.

## Launch standard

A successful launch means:
- the curriculum is real,
- the lessons feel useful,
- the activities create progression,
- the app can deliver the learning experience cleanly,
- and contributors can extend the system without confusion.

## Audience

The product is for beginners and early learners who want a real understanding of the market, not just surface-level finance content.

The user should leave smarter, more structurally aware, and better able to explain what price is doing.
